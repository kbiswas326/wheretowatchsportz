England Women got their ICC Women's T20 World Cup 2026 campaign off to a stunning start, posting 219 for 1 against Sri Lanka in Birmingham on June 12 before winning by 87 runs.

## Match Summary

England's batters put on an exhibition of power hitting on home soil, with the Edgbaston crowd in full voice. Their total of 219 for 1 was a statement of intent, and Sri Lanka never threatened in reply, bowled out for 132 in 20 overs. England's bowling was equally impressive, keeping Sri Lanka to a manageable total before ultimately wrapping up a comprehensive win.

## Tournament Context

The ICC Women's T20 World Cup 2026 is hosted in England and Wales, featuring 12 teams competing across Group 1 and Group 2. The tournament runs from June 12 to July 5, culminating in a final at Lord's.
